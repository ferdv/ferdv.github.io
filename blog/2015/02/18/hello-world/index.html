Hello, World!

Feb 18th, 2015 4:19 pm

For some time I have been contemplating the idea of starting my own blog. Usually, I have sent that idea to /dev/null fairly quickly, convincing myself that there are many blogs run by people that have something more interesting to say than me. Moreover, they probably present it to the world in a better way than I would be able to. This time I held the thought long enough to actually decide to give it a go. This post is an introduction as well as a statement of intent. You might want to skip it.

I think two things contributed to my decision to start a personal blog. First, I’ve discovered the Octopress blogging framework which looked so interesting that I wanted to find a use for it. The second inspiration came from the excellent blog of Matt Might and his post 6 blog tips for busy academics which robbed me of any remaining excuses for not trying. If find Matt’s collection of articles to be one of the most consistently interesting, highly readable and useful on the Internet. If you are interested in theory as well as practical aspects of programming languages, then definitely browse his blog and add his RSS feed to your reader now!

A part of my motivation for having a blog is to improve my writing and to collect notes on various topics. However, my hope is that I will be able to occasionally contribute material deemed interesting or useful by at least one person other than me. If you end up here while looking for a solution to some problem, or for a clarification on some subject and I manage to help you, then I will have succeeded.

The subjects I intend to touch in my posts are obviously based on my interests and should range over topics such as programming, theory and practice of programming languages, operating systems, Linux, computer science in general, and music. I might include some general rants about everyday annoyances.

Why “The Imp”? Well, I like what the Cambridge Advanced Learner’s Dictionary has to say about imp:

  • a small evil spirit

  • (often humorous) a child that behaves badly, but in a way that is funny rather than serious:

    “Come here, you little imp!”

Also, it is an abbreviation of impostor.
